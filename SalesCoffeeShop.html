About Datasets

This sample data module contains representative retail data from a fictional coffee chain. The source data is contained in an uploaded file named April Sales.zip. Source: IBM. We have created sample data for a fictional coffee shop chain with three locations in New York city. The chain has purchased IBM Cognos Analytics to identify factors that contribute to their success, and ultimately to make data-informed decisions. Amber and Sandeep are the co-founders of the coffee chain. They uploaded their data in a series of spreadsheets and created a data module. From that data, they designed an operations dashboard and a marketing dashboard.

Data

The data available is in the form of a relational table. There are nine tables:

  • Sales Receipts
  • Pastry Inventory
  • Sales Targets
  • Customer
  • Dates
  • Product
  • Sales Outlet
  • Staff
  • Generation

Use Case

Amber uses this dashboard to monitor her three stores.

On this dashboard, Amber can find out which items are frequently purchased by customers and can also find out the segmentation of buyers who visit the store. After knowing market segmentation, Amber and Sandeep can plan strategies to maximize sales.

For example, at the Lower Manhattan outlet, sales for that month did not meet the target.

From the dashboard data, Amber and Sandeep got an insight that the Lower Manhattan outlet had the most visitors in the Millennial and Gen Z age segments. The RFM score at this outlet was also small compared to the other two stores, this indicates that there are few "loyal customers".

Suggestion

So, Amber and Sandeep have developed a membership program to offer special promotions to attract old or inactive customers. Additionally, they plan to enhance facilities that align with the preferences of Gen Z and Millennials. To boost the appeal for new customers, they can create engaging content on social media and leverage influencers to reach their target market.
